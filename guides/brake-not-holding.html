Table Brake Not Holding?
Diagnosis & Parts Guide

A brake that won't hold the table stationary under cutting load is a safety issue. The W&B brake system has two main components: the hydraulic brake cylinder (WB25452) and the friction brake shoes (KB1124). Both are wear items and both are common failure points.

Symptoms

  • Table drifts under load: The table moves slowly when it should be stationary — the brake is not applying enough clamping force
  • Slow table stopping: The table takes noticeably longer to come to rest after disengaging the clutch
  • Hydraulic fluid visible around brake area: Leaking brake cylinder — the seals have failed
  • Grinding or squealing when brake applies: Brake shoes are worn to the backing plate, or contaminated with oil

Check the Brake Cylinder (WB25452)

The brake cylinder is a hydraulic actuator that applies clamping force to the brake shoes. Failure modes:
  • External leak: Visible hydraulic fluid around the cylinder — seals have failed. Replace the cylinder.
  • Internal bypass: No visible leak, but braking force is reduced — the piston seals are bypassing internally. The cylinder feels "spongy" when the brake is applied. Replace the cylinder.
  • Sticking: The cylinder does not fully extend or retract — corrosion or debris in the bore. Rebuild or replace.

Check the Brake Shoes (KB1124)

Brake shoes are the friction surfaces that contact the brake drum. Failure modes:
  • Worn friction material: Measure thickness — replace when worn to minimum. Sold as a matched pair.
  • Oil contamination: If the brake cylinder is leaking, hydraulic fluid contaminates the shoe friction material. Contaminated shoes will not hold — they must be replaced (not cleaned). Fix the cylinder leak first, or the new shoes will be destroyed quickly.
  • Glazing: The friction surface has become smooth and glassy from overheating. Shoes need replacement.
